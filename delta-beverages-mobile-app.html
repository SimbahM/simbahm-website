Project Overview

Introduction Banking in Zimbabwe is mobile-first. When CABS needed to ensure their digital experience matched their reputation as a leading financial institution, I stepped in to lead the UI/UX design of their mobile application.

The goal was simple but challenging: take complex financial transactions and make them feel effortless, secure, and modern.

My Role as a UI Designer

As the UI Designer on this project, my responsibilities included:

✅

Translating business requirements into visual design solutions

✅

Modernizing the look and feel of the app while maintaining brand consistency

✅

Designing intuitive, accessible, and clean user interfaces

✅

Creating high-fidelity mobile screens in Figma

✅

Building a reusable and scalable design system

✅

Preparing the final design for developer handoff

While UX strategy and flows were informed by existing banking standards, my primary focus was on visual design, layout structure, accessibility, and consistency across all screens.

My Role as a UI Designer
The Design Philosophy

I focused on three core pillars during the design process:

The goal was simple but challenging: take complex financial transactions and make them feel effortless, secure, and modern.

✅

Clarity over Clutter: Financial data can be overwhelming. I used clean typography, clear visual hierarchy, and white space to let the user breathe.

✅

Speed is Key: In Zimbabwe, you need to transact fast. I optimized user flows to ensure sending money or buying airtime happens in the fewest taps possible.

✅

Visual Trust: The UI needed to feel as secure as a bank vault. I used a professional, consistent design language to reassure users at every step.
